 MY WORK 

Indiana Secretary of State's Office

Summer Fellow (CS Intern)

April 2017 - Present

During the summer of 2017, I began working for the Indiana Secretary of State's Office as an intern. Some projects included automating certain tasks using Python, making back-end changes to certain web applications using C#, making UX and front-end changes to other certain web applications and managing and analysing large SQL Server databases used by the Secretary's bussiness, auto dealers and elections divisions.

The biggest and most memorable experience, however, was the Notary Public web application. The Notary application is used by every single Notary Public in the state of Indiana.

Indiana University School of Informatics, Computing and Engineering

Service Desk Consultant

March 2017 - Present

At the IU School of Informatics, Computing and Engineering, or SICE, I work in the IT support desk. There I provide top quality technical support to the entire schools staff, faculty and students. I manage, escalte and respond to user-submitted tickets, all while communicating with IT professionals on the issues.

Indiana University School of Informatics, Computing and Engineering

Undergraduate Instructor

August 2017 - Present

I am currently an Undergraduate Instructor for one of my favorite classes I've taken at IU. The course is taught by Saúl Blanco Rodríguez, and is a topics class about games and puzzles. The class explores game theory concepts, such as how to mathematically solve puzzles or win games. With this knowledge and refreshers on common path finding algorithms, students can either create their own games or implement existing ones, with competent AI, in Python.

I assist in lectures, labs and by answering any questions a student might have about the course, concepts beyond the course, the Python language, or anything else. I hold office hours twice a week and encourage students to visit me, as I love helping students with and discussing problems in the scope of the class.

You can learn more at the official course website, here.

Indiana University School of Informatics, Computing and Engineering

Research Intern

August 2016 - August 2017

During my sophomore year and some part of the following summer, I was working with a research group at IU called CNetS. I was tasked with creating a Twitter Bot that was able to recognize trending hashtags every day, and tweet a network graph about those hashtags. More information about these network graphs can be found be either visiting the OSoMe website or the Twitter bot itself.
